## The basics, confirmed

After years of speculation, Rockstar locked the details in the summer of 2026. GTA 6 launches on **November 19, 2026**, on PlayStation 5, PS5 Pro, and Xbox Series X and S. There is no PC version at launch, which is the detail driving a lot of the conversation in Zack's community.

There are two editions. The Standard edition runs **$79.99**, and the Ultimate edition runs **$99.99**. Pre-orders opened on June 25, 2026. For the confirmed pricing and edition breakdown, [Forbes covered the reveal in full](https://www.forbes.com/sites/paultassi/2026/06/24/rockstar-reveals-gta-6s-price-is-80-confirms-preorder-time-bonuses/), and [Fortune reported on the no-disc launch](https://fortune.com/2026/06/25/gta-6-release-date-price-no-disc-rockstar-games/) and the response to it.

## He called the price years ago

Here is the part longtime viewers already know: Zack saw the price coming. Back in a **December 2023 stream**, long before Rockstar said a word about cost, he predicted GTA 6 would be the game that pushed the industry toward triple-digit pricing.

> I think Grand Theft Auto 6 is going to set the frame for $100 games. It is going to come out, it is going to be $100, everybody is going to buy it, and then every game after that is going to be $100.
>
> — Asmongold, December 2023

He landed close. The Standard edition came in under his number at $79.99, but the **Ultimate edition hit the $100 mark he called**, and the wider point held: a major publisher tested a higher ceiling and the rest of the industry is watching to see if it sticks.

## On the console-only launch

The take that got picked up the most was about PC players getting left out at launch. Zack has been blunt about it, and equally blunt about the hardware itself. He put it plainly:

> Consoles are okay. But consoles are just kind of mediocre computers, really.
>
> — Asmongold

The frustration was never that the game looked bad. He actually said it looked way better than he expected. It was that the people running the most powerful gaming PCs in the world would be waiting while a weaker box got it first.
